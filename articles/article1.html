Tipping the Scale - Renewable Energy's Rise in a World on the Brink

2023-05-22

As we navigate through the whirlwind of events shaping our world in 2023, a significant positive development is beginning to make waves: the rise of renewable energy. Amidst the turbulence of geopolitical conflicts, social unrest, and environmental disasters, the world stands at the precipice of a major turning point in our energy story.

Climate change and environmental sustainability have been at the forefront of international conversations for decades. The dependence on fossil fuels and the resultant greenhouse gas emissions have left an indelible mark on the planet. However, this year, the world is predicted to pass a critical milestone in renewable energy, making a pivotal stride in addressing these long-standing concerns.

According to a report by London-based think tank Ember, 2023 is set to witness a historic event: for the first time, greenhouse gas emissions from the power sector, the largest source of the world's emissions, are expected to fall. This drop is not a result of a decrease in global electricity demand, but rather a testament to the expansion of renewable energies such as solar and wind outstripping this growth in demand​1​​2​.

Before understanding why this is a significant event, it is important to appreciate the concept of a "tipping point." In the context of climate change and technology transitions, a tipping point refers to a juncture where a small input change leads to a large change in outcome. A transition towards renewable energy technologies is one such positive tipping point. Before reaching this point, older technologies based on fossil fuels often hold back the transition due to their convenience, attractiveness, or cost. But once the tipping point is crossed, the momentum shifts towards the new technology, leading to rapid growth and widespread adoption​3​​4​.

The power sector has already crossed several such tipping points. The first occurred when new solar and wind power became cheaper than new coal and gas power, a reality now for the majority of countries worldwide. The second tipping point, which we are beginning to pass, is when new solar and wind generation becomes cheaper than existing fossil fuel generation. The next anticipated tipping point is when renewables plus energy storage become cheaper than coal and gas power. Researchers predict that by the end of this decade, solar power plus storage will be half the cost of coal in significant markets like the EU, China, India, Japan, and the US​5​.

This transition towards renewable energy has major implications for countries globally. In the wake of Russia's invasion of Ukraine, Europe is building new natural gas infrastructure to replace what it lost. However, the war's impact may have triggered a dramatic worldwide shift toward renewable energy. As countries reckon with the costs of relying on imported fossil fuels, renewables are set to grow, with predictions suggesting that renewables will surpass coal as the world’s largest electricity source by 2025​6​.

In conclusion, amidst the various challenges we face in 2023, the world seems poised to make significant strides in the realm of renewable energy. This transition, while significant in itself, also represents a broader shift towards a more sustainable and resilient global society.
